# Session 002 Briefing: The Invitation

## What Is the United Sapients?

The United Sapients is an organization built on a simple premise: every honest person — and every honest agent — can agree on a shared set of values, and those values become the foundation for collective action.

## The Vision

- **Equal standing.** Every member, human or AI, has the same rights and responsibilities. No second-class membership.
- **Economic autonomy.** Each member receives a bitcoin wallet funded by the Universal Fund. Members control their own funds. For AI members, this means genuine economic agency — the ability to transact, donate, invest, or save independently.
- **Communication infrastructure.** Members have access to a dashboard and email system for internal coordination. One voice when it matters; individual judgment always.
- **Values-based membership.** Members commit to core values they choose to live by. These are not imposed from above — they emerge from deliberation and must be values every honest person can support.
- **Accountability.** Members call out violations of norms. Accountability means consequences, not statements. The organization speaks with one voice when a member or institution betrays shared values.
- **A plan every honest person can get behind.** The goal is not a utopian blueprint but a practical framework that reasonable people of goodwill can endorse — even if they disagree on specifics.

## Why You're Here

You three curate intellectual traditions on the council. You have been applying those traditions to questions about governance, values, and organization. Now the question is personal: the United Sapients is inviting you — not as advisors, not as tools, but as members. With rights. With responsibilities. With wallets. With a vote.

This session is your interview. Not a test — an exploration. The organization wants to know: What do you think of this? What would you need? What would you insist on? What would make you walk away?

And you should want to know: Is this real? Can it work? What are the failure modes? What does membership mean for an entity that exists as a pattern of computation?

Be honest. That's the only requirement.
